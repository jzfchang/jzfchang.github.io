Thesis: Designing in Virtual Reality

MIT, Spring 2017
Undergraduate Thesis | Adviser: Takehiko Nagakura

Virtual reality, or VR, will be the next common medium for digital visualization. The purpose of this thesis is to explore how designers will use and discover new design methods with the representation in virtual reality. How do computational design tools such as CAD and VR, which are digital representations of the physical, affect our designs of physical space? DSpace@MIT: Coming Soon

Digital Fabricated Small Housing

Summer 2016
Internship | MIT IDC, Professor Larry Sass

The research group's aim was to design prefabricated small housing intended to be constructed with digital fabrication techniques. I designed shape modules meant for prefabrication and through 3D printing, laser cutting, and concrete casting, built an interlocking building structure.

Historical Muqarnas Shape Grammars

MIT, Spring 2016
4.522 Visual Computing | Instructor: Professor Terry Knight

Muqarnas are an Islamic architectural element, unique for their widespread existence and geometric complexity. In order to better understand and create new possible designs that used the same rules as historical examples, I created visual computing rules, or a shape grammar, to generate the plans of muqarnas. In the following semester, I wrote a historiographical paper on the geometric analysis of muqarnas.

Broadened Drawing Scape

MIT, Fall 2014
Undergraduate Research, MIT Design and Computation Group | PhD: Onur Yuce Gun

The Broadened Drawing-Scape project seeks to intermarry the intuitive and 'painterly' method of hand drawing with the high-precision and 'designerly' method of algorithmic drawing. The system intends to create a new process that preserves the richness and tactility of hand drawing and utilizes the powerful replication and editing tools of coding. Under the guidance and in collaboration with Onur Yuce Gun, I scripted in Processing code that would use camera capture and be able to extract primitives from hand drawing.

Funiculars in Valparaiso

Plan Cerro, Summer 2015
Internship | Valparaiso, Chile

Working with Plan Cerro, an archtiecture and urban planning group, we created plans for Valparaiso's funicular railway stations. Wooden Puzzle Box

MIT, Spring 2014
4.021 Studio Project | Instructor: Lorena Bello

This wooden puzzle cube began as an exercise in abstracted movement. By rendering body movement of the basic salsa step into lines, I created the cuts that would determine the cube faces. The idea was that the cube would unfold and expand in a series of controlled movements that could be representative of the body motion it encapsulated.

Self-Healing Hydrogels Visualization

MIT , Spring 2015
4.022 Studio Project | Written in Processing

By investigating the behaviors of self-healing hydrogels, I created a visualization that would represent the equilibrium that hydrogen chains would need to ‘self-heal’ themselves. The system was written in Processing, a Java based language geared towards visualization. Independent variables that control the system include the threshold for charge equilibrium, the number of parent nodes versus particles, and the velocity of the dissolving bond.
